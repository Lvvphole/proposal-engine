# Contractor Diagnostic Playbook

## Purpose

Qualify a contractor as a pilot candidate by diagnosing their current proposal workflow and quantifying revenue lost to slow follow-up.

## The Problem We Solve

Residential contractors lose 20-40% of quotable jobs in the 7–45 day window between receiving a supplier quote and delivering a proposal to the homeowner. The bottleneck is not estimation skill — it is the manual labor of converting supplier quotes into presentable proposals, multiplied across 10-30 active quotes at any time.

## Discovery Questions

### Volume and Velocity

1. **How many supplier quotes do you receive per week?** (Target: 5+ per week to justify the service)
2. **What is your average time from receiving a quote to sending a proposal?** (If < 48 hours, they may not need us. If > 7 days, strong fit.)
3. **How many quotes are sitting in your inbox or truck right now that haven't become proposals yet?** (Visceral question — makes the pain concrete)

### Revenue Impact

4. **What is your average job size?** (Target: $8K–$25K for residential roofing/remodeling)
5. **Of the quotes you receive, what percentage turn into proposals you actually send?** (Industry avg: 60-70%. Below 50% = strong signal.)
6. **Of the proposals you send, what percentage close?** (Industry avg: 30-40%. Combined with #5, this gives the conversion funnel.)

### Current Workflow

7. **Walk me through what happens after you get a quote from your supplier.** (Listen for: manual data entry, Excel/Word templates, copy-paste, "I get to it when I can")
8. **Who does the proposal work?** (Owner doing it themselves = biggest pain point. Dedicated estimator = less urgent but still valuable for throughput.)
9. **What does your proposal look like?** (Handwritten? Word doc? Formal template? This determines how much formatting value we add.)

## Qualification Scorecard

| Factor | Score 1 (Weak Fit) | Score 3 (Good Fit) | Score 5 (Strong Fit) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quote volume | < 3/week | 5-10/week | 10+/week |
| Quote-to-proposal time | < 48 hours | 3-7 days | > 7 days |
| Average job size | < $5K | $8-15K | $15K+ |
| Quote-to-proposal rate | > 80% | 50-70% | < 50% |
| Proposal creation | Dedicated estimator | Owner + template | Owner, ad hoc |

**Minimum viable pilot candidate:** Total score ≥ 15 (out of 25)

## Revenue Loss Calculator

```
Lost annual revenue =
  (quotes/week × 52)
  × (1 - quote_to_proposal_rate)
  × avg_job_size
  × close_rate
```

**Example:** 10 quotes/week, only 60% become proposals, $15K avg job, 35% close rate

```
= (10 × 52) × (1 - 0.60) × $15,000 × 0.35
= 520 × 0.40 × $15,000 × 0.35
= $1,092,000 in quotable pipeline lost annually
```

Even capturing 20% of that lost pipeline = $218K in additional revenue for the contractor.

At our 8% fee = $17,500 in annual revenue from this single contractor.

## Pilot Pitch

> "I can show you exactly how much revenue you're leaving on the table in that gap between getting a supplier quote and sending a proposal. For the first 30 days, I'll process your quotes and generate proposals at no cost. After that, you only pay when a proposal I generate actually closes. No monthly fee, no commitment — just results."

## Disqualifiers

Do not pilot with contractors who:

- Have average job sizes below $5K (unit economics don't work at 8%)
- Already have sub-24-hour turnaround (no pain to solve)
- Do primarily commercial/government work (different proposal requirements)
- Won't share quote documents (can't operate without input)
- Have fewer than 3 quotes per week (insufficient volume for meaningful data)
